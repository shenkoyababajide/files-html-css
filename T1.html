First Heading

The Bylaws herein shall be subject to, and governed by, the Nonprofit Corporation Act of North Carolina and the Articles of Incorporation of the Carolina Wolves Basketball Association. In the event of a direct conflict between the provisions of these Bylaws and that of the Nonprofit Corporation Act of North Carolina, the latter shall take precedence. Also, In the event of a direct conflict between the provisions of these Bylaws and the Articles of Incorporation, the former shall take precedence.

Second Heading

Members of the Board or Advisory Council shall not discuss or disclose information about the Association or its activities to any person or entity unless such information is already a matter of public knowledge, such person or entity has a need to know, or the disclosure of such information is in furtherance of the Associationâ€™s purposes, or can reasonably be expected to benefit the Association. Directors shall use their discretion and good judgment in discussing the affairs of the Association with third parties. Without limiting the foregoing, Directors may discuss upcoming fundraisers and the purposes and functions of the Association. Subsequent upon this, each Director or Advisory Council member shall execute a confidentiality agreement consistent herewith upon being voted onto and accepting appointment to serve.

Third Heading

The officers of the Association shall consist of a President, a Vice-President, a Secretary, a Treasurer, a President, Sports Director and such other subordinate officers as the Board may appoint or authorize the President to appoint. Two or more offices may be held by the same person, except that the President may not serve as the Secretary or Treasurer. No officer may act in more than one capacity where action of two or more officers is required.

Fourth Heading

In addition to the duties of Board members in Article III Section 1, officers shall carry out duties that pertain to their offices and other such duties which may be required by law, the Articles of Incorporation, or by these Bylaws, contingent upon the control of the Board, and they shall perform any other such additional duties which the Board may assign to them at its discretion. All officers must have the status of active members of the Board.

Fifth Heading

The Board may, by the resolution adopted by a majority of the Directors then in office, designate Standing and Ad Hoc Committee for smooth running of the Association. Any committee, to the extent provided in the resolution of the Board, shall have all the authority of the Board. Each such committee shall consist of one (1) or more directors and may also include persons who are not on the Board but whom the directors believe to be reliable and competent to serve on a specific committee. However, committees exercising any authority of the Board may not have any non-director members.

Sixth Heading

Early registration will be made available from our website beginning no sooner than three months prior to the first official day of practice, and the registration shall be deemed complete upon In-person registration and submission of necessary documents and payment of dues prior to the first day of practice. If a parent/guardian withdraws their child(ren) thirty days prior to the first official day of practice, a two-third refund of the registration fee will be offered provided that all equipment provided has been returned. If a parent/guardian withdraws their child(ren) during the tournament season, the withdrawal shall be considered complete only after all standing debts have been settled and equipment returned and there shall be no refund of registration, donation or any form of support previously made.
